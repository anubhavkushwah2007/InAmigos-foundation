How It Became

A journey from local volunteer circles to a structured, nationally recognized corporate non-profit entity.

Origins & Governance Framework

The InAmigos Foundation was established on September 23, 2020, by its Founder & CEO, Mr. Govind Shukla. Initiated as a targeted, localized grassroots project in Chhattisgarh, the organization quickly proved its execution framework and expanded. Today, it operates widespread active volunteer nodes spanning multiple Indian states, including Haryana and West Bengal.

To support high-level governance, corporate transparency, and strategic growth pipelines, the organization operates with complete central framework validation:

How It Works

Bypassing high logistical overhead by relying directly on digital workflows and community micro-economies.

The Volunteer Ecosystem

Operations are decentralized. InAmigos coordinates broad networks of localized professionals and students managed seamlessly under dedicated regional Volunteer Supervisors to implement fast actions on the ground.

Localized Sourcing Philosophy

The foundation assesses community needs dynamically, sources raw materials from independent regional vendors, and employs local tailors for production, keeping capital directly within regional micro-economies.

Youth Professional Pipeline

The operational engine is sustained by an extensive internship ecosystem. Thousands of young people across India take on real administrative duties—handling digital workflows, copywriting, research, and data processing.

Current Projects & Core Purpose

Six specialized structural portfolios targeted at providing lasting social intervention pipelines.

Humanitarian Relief

Project Seva

Core Purpose

To eliminate sudden food insecurity, baseline hunger, and clothing deprivation in urban slums, rural pockets, and migrant camps.

Operational Method

Volunteers prepare and distribute fresh, nutritious meals and provide seasonal clothing items and clean uniforms directly to children.

Child Education

Project Bachpanshala

Core Purpose

To close structural education and technical gaps for kids relying on resource-constrained public or low-income schooling systems.

Operational Method

Delivers systematic after-school learning environments focusing on standard core academics alongside digital literacy and life skills.

Women Empowerment

Project Udaan

Core Purpose

To break historical poverty trends by giving remote and village-based women direct agency, functional trade options, and self-reliance.

Operational Method

Organizes vocational training loops in tailoring, local handicrafts, and business management alongside health workshops.

Animal Welfare

Project Jeev

Core Purpose

Providing sustained baseline nutrition, street-level care, and safety frameworks for speech-deprived stray animals left vulnerable.

Operational Method

Establishes community-managed feeding stations and structures functional makeshift outdoor shelters for street dogs, cats, and cows.

Sustainability

Project Prakriti

Core Purpose

To actively combat local deforestation, promote community eco-habits, and drive direct, measurable environmental climate action.

Operational Method

Brings together local volunteer networks to manage large-scale native sapling tree plantation blocks and waste education drives.

Skill Development

Project Vikas

Core Purpose

To fast-track professional competence and digital capabilities for young students and entry-level individuals joining the modern workforce.

Operational Method

Designs and runs practical, remote professional internships that immerse candidates in digital marketing, social research, and media operations.
